### Ivy

`Core Contributor - ML | Jul '23 - Oct '23`

Though the product vision looks quite different right now, when I had joined Ivy, it was a group of very
passionate and cracked people trying `to solve the problem of the fragmentation of the ML stack`. To give the short version,
there are multiple options available for ML frameworks, languages, compilers, cloud, basically the whole stack and every
enterprise and organisation prefers its own way of doing things which makes collaborations between research labs much harder.

With Ivy, you could just `convert the backend of a library from TensorFlow to PyTorch` and quickly test the latest research
from Google without having to spend significant time rewriting the code in your preferred framework. This is similar to 
Keras 3.0 where you can run your code in the "backend" of your choice while the "frontend" remains Keras. With Ivy, the
"frontend" could be your choice too.

I fell for the codebase, the community and the developers here much before I applied to a role. Eventually I joined as a Core Contributor.
My major tasks were to add more functionalities to the "frontend" and the "backend" of all ML frameworks (either wrapped 
around Ivy, or wrapping Ivy). This is where my ML fundamentals strengthened as I had to look into the `implementations 
of fundamental functions across TensorFlow, PyTorch, PaddlePaddle, MindSpore, MxNet and Numpy`.

In case you're a beginner in ML, I would suggest starting with either Jax or PyTorch-Lightning, you'll develop a steady 
grip on your fundamentals.

This is also the place where I had my `first code reviews, first glimpse into graph compilers and how frameworks are designed,
first time writing library/model tutorials, working with CI/CD pipelines and Hypothesis testing, and integrating Ivy
into larger codebases.`

I eventually felt a need to upskill since my major responsibilities could have been automated by a fine-tuned LLM and it is
not that hard to add to frameworks. Still, I have seen very limited codebases and open-source organisations which are as 
beautiful as Ivy was at that time and my time here opened me up to `beautiful opportunities and amazing people`.

_(now [Unify.ai](https://unify.ai/) and [Ivy.dev](https://www.ivy.dev/))_
